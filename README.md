Pragmatic AI Agents: Engineering Beyond Illusion

More and more practitioners are beginning to recognize that the first wave of “Agentic 2025” systems did not truly deliver. The industry is already shifting toward what some are calling “Agentic 2026” — a quieter, more structural rethink.

In hindsight, AI agent development over the last few years resembles alchemy.

We combined powerful ingredients — LLMs, embeddings, vector stores — and through careful staging, produced systems that looked like gold.

But appearance is not architecture.
And simulation is not system design.

Yet alchemy was not useless. It developed laboratory techniques, experimental habits, material knowledge, and a language of transformation. Those prerequisites later enabled chemistry — when systematic, pragmatic scientists replaced symbolic interpretation with reproducible method.

Today’s prompt engineering tricks and naive RAG pipelines may represent a similar pre-architectural phase. They created awareness. They attracted funding. They surfaced real capability. They helped us understand what is possible.

But real AI agents will not emerge from better staging. They will emerge from structure — from decision loops, constrained tool use, state management, verification, and evaluation rigor.

The alchemical phase of AI agents has done its job.
Now the chemistry must begin.
